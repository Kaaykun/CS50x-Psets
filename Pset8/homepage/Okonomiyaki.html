Okonomiyaki, the savory Japanese pan-fried pancake

Hiroshima Style Okonomiyaki

Hiroshima style, or “Kasane-yaki” (repeatedly baked) is where the batter is first spread on the iron plate or frypan, and ingredients are added onto it one by one. The layers are typically batter, cabbage, pork, and yakisoba. Optional items such as squid, octopus, dried bonito flakes, and other seafood, as well as nori flakes or powder, mung bean sprouts, egg, chicken, cheese, and other ingredients, depending on the preferences of the cook and the customer. Noodles (yakisoba, udon) are also used as a topping with fried egg and a generous amount of okonomiyaki sauce.

Kansai Style Okonomiyaki

Kansai style, or “Maze-yaki” is where the ingredients are all mixed together with the batter and then pan-fried. Usually the batter will contain flour, yam, water, eggs, shredded cabbage, green onions and meat (most often thinly sliced pork). Of course, the recipe depends highly on the chef and a menu of an Okonomiyaki restaurant will in general also contain seafood, vegetarian and different meat options.
